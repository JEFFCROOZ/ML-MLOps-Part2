# 10 — Copy QA Checklist (Part 2)

This checklist is to be completed before any module copy goes into code. Review each item for every page.

---

## Voice & Tone

- [ ] Written in second person ("you," not "the user" or "the learner")
- [ ] No jargon used without explanation
- [ ] No passive voice ("ratings are used by the model" → "the model uses ratings")
- [ ] Sentences are under 20 words where possible
- [ ] No condescending language ("simply," "obviously," "just")
- [ ] No filler phrases ("In today's fast-paced world...")
- [ ] Tone is warm but peer-level — not talking down, not talking up

---

## Content Accuracy

- [ ] No factual errors in technical descriptions
- [ ] No tool or vendor presented as the only valid option
- [ ] No claims that concepts only apply at enterprise scale
- [ ] All code snippets and artifact examples are plausible and realistic
- [ ] Metrics and thresholds used in examples are reasonable (not cherry-picked to look impressive)
- [ ] The MovieLens example is used consistently — not a different dataset mid-module

---

## Continuity with Part 1

- [ ] No re-teaching of concepts covered in Part 1 (RMSE, SVD, train/test split, cosine similarity)
- [ ] The bridge on the Welcome page clearly connects to where Part 1 ended
- [ ] Module 6 (The Living System) references specific modules from Part 1 accurately
- [ ] The link to Part 1 on the Welcome page is functional

---

## Bias & Fairness Check

- [ ] No implicit assumption that the learner works at a large company
- [ ] No implicit assumption that the learner has a team — solo practitioners are addressed
- [ ] No language that privileges any specific tool, cloud provider, or framework
- [ ] No assumptions about the learner's background, gender, or geography
- [ ] Hands-on exercises are achievable without paid infrastructure

---

## Structure Check (each module)

- [ ] Follows the five-section template: What's Happening → Why It Matters → Example → Hands-On → Key Takeaway
- [ ] Key Takeaway is a single sentence that could stand alone
- [ ] Hands-on section has at least 2-3 concrete steps
- [ ] At least one realistic artifact per module (log entry, dashboard, alert, registry entry)
- [ ] No module exceeds what a reader could comfortably absorb in 15 minutes

---

## Legal & Compliance

- [ ] No financial or investment advice
- [ ] No claims that the course output is production-ready without user validation
- [ ] All external references are to publicly available resources
- [ ] No offensive, discriminatory, or disparaging language
